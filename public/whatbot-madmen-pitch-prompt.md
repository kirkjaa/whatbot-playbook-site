## SECTION D — TONE AND STYLE INSTRUCTIONS

Apply these throughout every slide:

1. **Never use "platform" without an adjective.** It is always "The Venture Building OS" or "the production platform" — never just "platform."

2. **Every statistic lives inside a sentence.** Never bullet a number alone. The 55% mango rejection rate is more powerful as "55% of every Thai mango harvest is rejected for looking wrong" than as "55% rejection rate."

3. **The GETHAC story is the anchor.** Reference it at least once in every third slide — not as a case study box, but as the emotional truth that everything else proves.

4. **Operate at Level 4 of the Emotional Benefit Ladder on every slide.** Features are mentioned, never presented. The emotional and identity layers are always visible.

5. **The investor is the hero, always.** Every slide asks implicitly: "What does this mean for the investor?" before "What does this say about Whatbot?"

6. **One STAR Moment per slide — designed deliberately.** Ask: "What is the one thing from this slide they will quote to their partner on the way home?" Design that moment. Make it the headline or the closing line.

7. **Vulnerability appears once, early, and authentically.** The GETHAC delivery story — "we weren't sure we could do it in six weeks. We did it in five." — is the rep's personal photo in the Kodak Carousel.

8. **The closing line of the entire pitch echoes the opening.** The mango. The carousel completes.
