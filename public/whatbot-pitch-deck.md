# SLIDE 2 · THE PROBLEM

## Venture building is the most expensive, fragmented, and time-consuming process in business.

Whether you're a Thai farmer cooperative turning B-grade durian into a beauty brand, or a Singapore venture studio building its 8th portfolio company this year — the process is the same.

**And it is completely broken.**

### The Fragmentation Tax

| What needs to happen | Current approach | Cost | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Market research & sizing | Manual research + consultant | $2,000 – $10,000 | 1–3 weeks |
| Competitive intelligence | Specialized firm | $5,000 – $25,000 | 2–4 weeks |
| Financial modeling | Excel + CFO consultant | $5,000 – $20,000 | 2–4 weeks |
| Go-to-market strategy | Marketing agency | $3,000 – $15,000 | 3–6 weeks |
| Brand assets & visuals | Freelancers + tools | $1,000 – $5,000 | 1–2 weeks |
| Investor pitch deck | Designer + strategist | $2,000 – $8,000 | 1–2 weeks |
| MVP prototyping | Dev shop | $10,000 – $50,000 | 4–12 weeks |
| **TOTAL** | **10+ tools · 5+ vendors** | **$28,000 – $133,000** | **14–33 weeks** |

### Three Compounding Pains

**Pain 1 — The Context Tax**
Every time work moves from the market researcher to the financial modeler to the deck designer, context is lost. The financial model doesn't reflect the market research. The pitch deck doesn't use the competitive intelligence. Teams spend **40% of their time coordinating between tools**, not building.

**Pain 2 — The Expertise Gap**
General AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) can answer questions. They cannot run a 7-phase competitive intelligence pipeline, generate a DCF model, and turn it into a pitch deck — in the same session, with shared context, automatically.

**Pain 3 — The Studio Scale Problem**
800+ venture studios globally each run the same research → validate → model → pitch cycle 3–10 times per year. They cannot systematize it. Every venture starts from zero.
